AN EXPLORATION OF ELECTRICITY CONSUMPTION PATTERNS

This project analyzes daily electricity consumption data from Indian states between January 2019 and December 2020.

  • State-wise and region-wise consumption patterns analyzed
  • Special focus on COVID-19 lockdown impact (March-June 2020)
  • Interactive Tableau dashboards for exploring consumption trends

India is the world's third-largest producer and third-largest consumer of electricity. The national electric grid in India has an installed capacity of 370.106 GW as of 31 March 2020. Renewable power plants, which also include large hydroelectric plants, constitute 35.86% of India's total installed capacity. During the fiscal year (FY) 2019–20, the total electricity generation in the country was 1,598 TWh, of which 1,383.5 TWh generated by utilities. The gross electricity consumption per capita in FY2019 was 1,208 kWh. In 2015-16, electric energy consumption in agriculture was recorded as being the highest (17.89%) worldwide. The per capita electricity consumption is low compared to most other countries despite India having a low electricity tariff. Through this analysis, I've explored how consumption patterns vary across states, regions, and time periods, with particular attention to the effects of the 2020 lockdown on energy demand.
